Methodology and caveats

What's classified, with which classifier

Two corpora, two classifiers. The Drive reading library (1,238 PDFs curated by hand) was read full-text — title, abstract, introduction, and methods — by Claude Haiku 4.5 with a structured classification prompt covering all nine categories plus tag dimensions. This is the headline analysis on which the metrics, trends, modality breakdown, and exemplars on this dashboard are based.

The PubMed broad sweep is the broader literature scoped via NCBI E-utilities, restricted to 2000–2026 and to neuroimaging × race/ethnicity terminology. Its 4,887 abstracts were classified by a regex heuristic and hand-validated on a 78-paper stratified sample (estimated 75–85% accuracy on substantive categories). The PubMed numbers are useful as context for the broader literature, but the Haiku Drive analysis is the more reliable signal on the proportional question.

Search strategy (PubMed sweep)

Search ran against NCBI PubMed via E-utilities on May 14, 2026. The query unions MeSH headings and title/abstract terms for neuroimaging and race/ethnicity:

("Neuroimaging"[MeSH] OR "Magnetic Resonance Imaging"[MeSH] OR "Positron-Emission Tomography"[MeSH] OR "Electroencephalography"[MeSH] OR "Magnetoencephalography"[MeSH] OR "Spectroscopy, Near-Infrared"[MeSH] OR "Diffusion Tensor Imaging"[MeSH] OR neuroimaging[TIAB] OR "magnetic resonance imaging"[TIAB] OR fMRI[TIAB] OR "diffusion tensor"[TIAB] OR "positron emission tomography"[TIAB] OR electroencephalography[TIAB] OR magnetoencephalography[TIAB] OR "near-infrared spectroscopy"[TIAB]) AND ("Racial Groups"[MeSH] OR "Ethnicity"[MeSH] OR race[TIAB] OR racial[TIAB] OR ethnicity[TIAB] OR ethnic[TIAB]) AND ("2000"[PDAT] : "2026"[PDAT])

Databases consulted: PubMed only. Full text was not retrieved for the PubMed sweep; for the Drive corpus, full PDF text was extracted from a synced local copy of the user's curated Race_Coef folder.

Classification scheme (nine mutually exclusive categories)

Substantive uses: primary variable (race or ethnic group is the variable being examined, e.g., own-race face perception, Black-White medical comparisons); disparities causal (race-linked exposures like discrimination, structural racism, neighborhood disadvantage, SES, or childhood adversity modeled as mediators of brain differences); disparities descriptive (group differences with disparities framing but no causal mechanism); methodological critique (the paper interrogates how race is used in neuroimaging research itself — atlas bias, AI fairness, sample representation).

Adjustment uses: nuisance covariate (race entered in statistical models without interpretation); matching variable (race used to balance comparison groups, not modeled).

Incidental: sample descriptor only (race appears in the demographics table but not in any analysis); false positive (incidental mention, e.g., "pan-ethnic disorder" phrasing in a methods paper); cannot determine.

Why Haiku full-text is more reliable than abstract regex

The regex classifier reads only the abstract, so it routinely misses the "adjusted for age, sex, race/ethnicity" sentence that lives in the methods section. On the Drive corpus, Haiku reads the first seven pages of each PDF — including the methods — and finds nuisance-covariate use in 34% of papers vs. 12% for the regex. The same gap likely exists in the broad PubMed sweep: the true rate of nuisance-covariate usage there is almost certainly also around 30%, but cannot be confirmed without running Haiku on those 4,887 abstracts too. A script to do that (classify_pubmed_with_haiku.py) is saved alongside this dashboard and can be run locally with an Anthropic API key.

Files and reproducibility

All processing scripts and data are saved in the working folder: pull_corpus.py (PubMed pull), classify.py (regex classifier), classify_with_haiku.py (Drive Haiku run), classify_pubmed_with_haiku.py (PubMed Haiku run, ready to execute), corpus.jsonl, classified.jsonl, drive_classified.jsonl, dashboard_data.json, drive_dashboard_data.json.

Data: NCBI PubMed E-utilities (n=4,887, 2000–2026) and a curated reading library of 1,238 PDFs.

Classifications: regex heuristic (PubMed, abstract-only) and Claude Haiku 4.5 (Drive, full-text). Hand-validated on a 78-paper stratified sample.
